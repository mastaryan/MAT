Coach Erik Hagen

Director of Development | Prospects 18U Head Coach

Erik Hagen coached college baseball for 16 years, including head coaching positions at both Transylvania University (1997-2000) and Georgetown College (2004-09). He has served as an assistant coach at Emory (1995), Georgia Tech (1996), Cincinnati (1997) and Ohio State (2002-04). A native of Lima, Ohio, Hagen was a team captain and four-year letterman at Wooster College, where he received a bachelor’s degree in history. He was a two-time first-team all conference selection and was the team’s most valuable player as a senior in 1993. Hagen signed professionally with the Frontier League and played for the Chillicothe Paints Professional Baseball Club in 1993. He later added master’s degrees from Eastern Kentucky and Union College.

Currently, Hagen, who has Master's Degrees from EKU and Union College, teaches special education at the Martin Luther King Academy for Excellence in Fayette County Public Schools, a position he has held since 2011. He also has taught for Laurel County Public Schools and was an after-school tutor and mentor for Urban Impact in Lexington in 2010. Hagen lives in Lexington, Ky. with his wife Natasha, three daughters and one son.
